Bukit Tinggi - A hidden gem near genting highlands

3 weeks before the national day long weekend, I was struggling to come up with somewhere to go. Most of the people I know weren't free during that period but luckily my friend sze kai was willing to take a day leave to accompany me. So after a quick discussion, we decided to go to Bukit tinggi.

Bukit tinggi is a hill just a little down the highway from genting Highlands. At 800m, it is not high enough for montane forests but is nevertheless very productive for birding. We encountered frequent bird waves in the morning and evenings, and even in the afternoons there were still bulbuls fluttering about. The main birding areas are around the Japanese garden, tho the road from the hotel is decent too.

Accommodation

Since we both don't drive, we stayed in Colmar Tropicale, a french themed village in Bukit tinggi 3km away from the main birding area. It costed about 200rm for one night + 13rm Pahang tax. Quite expensive considering the condition of the hotel, but wasn't too painful to the wallet.

Transport

We managed to get a grab from the city for 80rm. The hotel also provides shuttle bus service from berjaya times square to Colmar tropicale and back, 38rm per person. *insert timetable* There is also an hourly shuttle from the village to Japanese garden free of charge, from 10am to 4pm.

Food

There are a few restaurants, a 7 eleven and Starbucks at Colmar tropicale but we didn't visit any of them as they had terrible reviews on Google. We had lunch and dinner at a restaurant at The Chateau Spa & Resorts further up which had generous portions and tasty food for only a few dollars more expensive (we only tried nasi goreng).

Birding

We easily got the ferruginous partridge and mountain peacock pheasant at the hide without needing to wait long. Buff-breasted babblers and ochraceous bulbuls frequent the feeding stations looking for an easy meal. From here is where we heard our first orange-breasted trogon and green broadbill following a bird wave. We eventually got decent views of the trogon and "record shot" quality shots of the broadbill along the road up to the hide. Another target of the trip was the Sunda scimitar babbler which we saw 2 times following bird waves but were incredibly difficult to photograph due to their hyperactive nature. ----------------------- At the entrance of the Japanese garden was a clearing with a few fruiting trees which attracted many bulbuls, like the scaly-breasted and gray-bellied bulbuls, and a few Verditer flycatchers. Barbets were also oddly plentiful at this location, perhaps they were eyeing the dead trees to build their nests. We saw sooty, blue-eared, gold-whiskered and yellow-crowned barbets. While we were scanning bird waves, sze kai spotted a very rare helmeted hornbill perched far far away.

Spendings

Spent a total of about 250sgd. Could have saved more if there were more people or if we chose to save more on food. 80sgd changi - KLIA 30sgd KLIA - senai
